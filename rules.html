The Rules of Rational Engagement

Unlike traditional forums where comments disintegrate into emotional chaos, Talking Too Much enforces structured argumentation nodes. To ensure every debate stays logical, coherent, and toxicity-free, we demand absolute adherence to our five structural rules.

Enter Chambers

Arguments must focus exclusively on the claim, the data, or the logical structure of the opposing side. Personal insults, demographic attacks, and mocking statements will be instantly flagged by our community-moderated algorithm. Three validated flags result in automatic session suspension.

Any argument making explicit claims of fact, physical values, historical data, or corporate statistics must include a verified reference source. This can be in the form of academic links, direct book references, or governmental research URLs. Unsubstantiated "facts" will be marked as speculative.

While we support talking about artificial intelligence, we do not tolerate machine-generated copy-paste arguments meant to flood debate chambers. Arguments must reflect human synthesis. Submitting long ChatGPT outputs without editing or direct integration violates the chamber's creative ownership policies.

Active debates are governed by a dynamic clock. However, if a debate chamber registers more than 90% consensus on either side for 48 consecutive hours with over 1,000 total votes, the chamber achieves "Stable Consensus" and is permanently locked. The winning side's argument nodes are archived as a cultural factoid.

Members have the absolute authority to rate other arguments. An argument node can be upvoted for "Cohesion," "Politeness," or "Factual Accuracy." High-rated arguments rise to the top of the debate pillars. Low-rated or continuously downvoted nodes are compressed and collapsed under "Unsound Logic."

Quick Handbook

  • Be Concise: The most upvoted arguments are typically between 100 to 250 words. Focus on a single core point.
  • Be Polite: Disagreement is the catalyst for growth, but disrespect is the tool of the logically defeated.
  • Be Accurate: If you cite a data point, summarize the source directly so peers can verify it with a single click.

Disciplinary Measures

We strictly protect our environment. Users who continuously post insults or deliberate misinformation will receive automated bans:

  • • 1st Violation: 24-Hour Posting Ban
  • • 2nd Violation: 7-Day Chamber Suspension
  • • 3rd Violation: Permanent IP Block
